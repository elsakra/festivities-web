Everyone wants a number. "How many hours? How many months?" The honest answer is: it depends — but we can give you a much better answer than the vague responses you usually get.

## The Official Answer

The US Foreign Service Institute (FSI) — which trains diplomats to speak languages professionally — classifies Spanish as a **Category I language** for English speakers. Their estimate: **600-750 hours** of study to reach professional working proficiency (roughly B2/C1 level on the CEFR scale).

That sounds like a lot. But let's break it down:

- **At 30 minutes/day**: about 3-4 years
- **At 1 hour/day**: about 18-24 months
- **At 2 hours/day**: about 9-12 months

But here's the thing: those estimates assume traditional classroom-style study — textbooks, exercises, grammar drills. **Conversational practice is dramatically more efficient.**

## Why Conversation Changes Everything

The research on language acquisition is clear: **meaningful output** (producing language in real communication) accelerates learning far faster than passive study or exercise-based repetition.

When you have a real conversation — even with an AI — your brain is forced to:

1. Retrieve vocabulary under time pressure
2. Apply grammar rules in context (not abstractly)
3. Repair misunderstandings in real time
4. Process and respond to new input

This kind of active engagement creates stronger neural connections than passive study. It's why people often learn more Spanish in one week of immersion than in a year of classes.

## Realistic Milestones with Daily Practice

Here's what most learners report with **30-60 minutes of daily conversational practice** (using Festivities or similar):

| Timeframe | What you can typically do |
|-----------|--------------------------|
| 2 weeks | Basic greetings, introduce yourself, count, order food |
| 1 month | Hold simple conversations about daily life |
| 2-3 months | Travel confidently, understand most of what you hear in slow speech |
| 4-6 months | Have extended conversations, express opinions, discuss plans |
| 8-12 months | Understand native speakers in everyday contexts, discuss complex topics |
| 2+ years | Near-native fluency, including slang, humor, and cultural nuance |

**Most people consider themselves "conversational" at around 4-6 months** with daily practice. That's the goal most learners are actually after — not professional diplomacy-level fluency.

## The Variables That Matter Most

### 1. How you practice

This is the biggest factor by far. **Flashcard apps build vocabulary. Conversation builds fluency.** If you spend 500 hours drilling vocabulary and grammar exercises, you'll know a lot about Spanish but struggle to actually speak it. If you spend 300 hours in real (or AI-simulated) conversations, you'll speak far more naturally.

### 2. Whether you already know a Romance language

If you speak French, Italian, or Portuguese, your Spanish timeline is roughly **halved**. These languages share 80-90% of vocabulary roots and very similar grammar structures.

### 3. Consistency vs. intensity

Daily practice — even 20 minutes — beats occasional marathon sessions. Your brain consolidates language learning during sleep, so spreading practice over days is more effective than cramming.

### 4. What "fluent" means to you

Are you learning to:
- Order food on a trip to Mexico City? (1-2 months)
- Chat comfortably with Spanish-speaking friends? (3-6 months)
- Work professionally in Spanish? (1-2 years)
- Be mistaken for a native? (5+ years, maybe never)

Set the right target and measure your progress against it.
